Visualizing Cattle Operations at Mountain Springs Ranch

An interactive spatiotemporal visualization showing how cattle groups move across ranch locations over the course of 2025.

Project Overview

Mountain Springs Ranch is a large cow-calf cattle operation in central Idaho with herds spread across many non-contiguous locations. Different land units are used seasonally, and different herds specialize in different cattle types.

Our goal was to create an interactive visualization that helps users understand where cattle are located on any given day, how herd distribution changes over time, and how ranch operations evolve spatially and seasonally.

Interactive Visualization

This interactive visualization shows seasonal herd distribution at Mountain Springs Ranch and illustrates how cattle move across ranch locations over time. By combining geography with animation, it helps users understand when and where different cattle groups are concentrated throughout the year.

Key Insights

Seasonal Redistribution

Cattle shift across ranch units over the course of the year, reflecting seasonal grazing patterns and operational logistics.

Location Specialization

Different sites support different cattle types, making the ranch’s operational geography easier to interpret.

Daily Ranch State

Transforming narrative movement data into a complete daily state table made reliable day-by-day visualization possible.

Design and Data Process

Data Pipeline

We began with a range manager narrative and a ranch valuation map. The original data was event-based and did not directly capture full herd state on each day.

  • Extract movement events from the narrative
  • Build daily observed herd records
  • Expand to a complete machine-readable daily state table
  • Visualize cattle type, location, and head count through time

Design Evolution

Our design evolved from simple movement logs toward an interactive map-based temporal view that better communicates both geography and seasonality.

  • Early focus on raw movement events
  • Need for a true daily ranch snapshot
  • Map-centered design to anchor users spatially
  • Date slider and playback controls for temporal exploration

Usability Testing

We conducted usability testing to understand whether users could correctly interpret the map, time controls, cattle categories, and daily herd distribution.

What We Tested

  • Can users identify where cattle are on a selected date?
  • Can users interpret dot counts and cattle types?
  • Can users understand seasonal changes over time?

What We Changed

  • Improved labeling and legend clarity
  • Made time controls more prominent
  • Refined map readability and explanatory text
